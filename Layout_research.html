The History of Neuroplasticity

For much of our medical history, doctors and scientists believed that our brains remain largely unchanged after infancy. In 1977, a paper was published declaring that neuroplasticity, plasticity of the brain and neural pathways, was possible in youths. By 1991, researchers began to understand that adult brains and neurological systems were also capable of change.
